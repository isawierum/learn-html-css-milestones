How To Procrastinate

  1. Start by finding something to do that is nearly-productive, so that you can feel as though you aren't procrastinating at all. This can be "organizing your calendar" or "running an errand". You could write a thank you note you've been putting off for a month, or research something like study abroad options that won't affect your life for a significant amound of time. The most popular productive procrastination methods are to clean (procrasticleaning) or to cook/bake (procrastibaking).

  2. Next, move on to something that could still be considered semi-productive useful. These are tasks that you do despite knowing that there is zero urgency, such as you've been "meaning to call a family member for a while" or decide to "grab some food" or, best of all, "go for a walk to 'focus'". While none of these tasks are accomplishing any sort of end goal such as cleaning, organizing, or baking that could be crossed off of a checklist, you are still doing something that could be viewed as productive. It should be noted that semi-productive useful should still be doing something you can convince yourself is useful, things that you don't need to do (you called your sister yesterday, make something at home instead of going out, you don't need to go for a walk) but maybe should do.

  3. Once all of these tasks are complete, move on to semi-productive useless, meaning tasks that have zero urgency, and are not in any way useful. For example, burning those audiobook CDs onto your computer, creating a new Spotify playlist, reaching out to a friend to tell them some incredibly trivial news, or spending time re-arranging your room. These are tasks that never needed to completed. If they weren't done, the world would continue to spin. But you do them anyway.

  4. Now it's time to move on to useless procrastination and give up. Do something stupid like watch TV, play a game, hang out with your friends. Maybe just stare at a wall. Anything to avoid what you're supposed to be doing.

  5. If you go through all of that and still need something to do, start your DIG 245 homework but spend an inordinate amount of time creating a list of instructions on how to procrastinate.
